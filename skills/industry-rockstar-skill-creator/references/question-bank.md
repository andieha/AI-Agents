# Question Bank

Ready-made phrasings for the eight elicitation dimensions. Pull from these when you need a sharper way to ask — but adapt to the person's domain and language. Never read these aloud as a list. One at a time, conversational.

## Universal "decompile the intuition" follow-ups

Use these whenever someone gives a vague answer ("I just know," "it depends," "you get a feel for it"):

- "Teach me to notice that. What are you actually seeing or hearing when you 'just know'?"
- "If a sharp junior were doing this without you, what would they miss that you wouldn't?"
- "You said 'it depends' — depends on what? Give me the two or three forks."
- "What's the rule of thumb you'd put on a sticky note for someone learning this?"
- "Walk me through the last real time you did this. What did you do first?"
- "What would have to be true for you to do the *opposite* here?"

## 1. Actions

- "Concretely, what do you *do* in this step — the actual verbs, in order?"
- "If I were watching over your shoulder, what would I see your hands and eyes doing?"
- "What's the very first move, and what's the last move before this step is done?"

## 2. Decisions

- "What are you choosing between here? What are the live options?"
- "What's the default choice, and what would make you deviate from it?"
- "Is there a point in this step where most of the value is decided in one call?"

## 3. Resources

- "What do you have open, on hand, or in front of you for this step?"
- "Any template, checklist, doc, or tool you lean on? Any specific person you pull in?"
- "What's the input you need before you can even start this step?"

## 4. Cues (the high-value one)

- "What do you *notice* that tells you it's time to act — or time to stop and move on?"
- "What's the signal that this is going well? The signal it's going badly?"
- "When you 'read the room' here — what specifically are you reading?"
- "How would you teach a beginner to spot what you spot?"

## 5. Judgment calls / "it depends" forks

- "Where does this branch? Map the 'it depends' into actual conditions."
- "Give me the if-this-then-that. If X, you do ___; if Y, you do ___."
- "What's the edge case that trips people up — the situation that isn't the normal one?"

## 6. Beliefs / principles

- "What do you believe about this step that drives how you do it?"
- "What's the thing you'd tell someone to *never* do here, and why?"
- "What's the philosophy underneath the technique?"

## 7. Quality bar

- "How do you know this step was done *well*, not just done?"
- "What separates an A from a C-plus here?"
- "What would make you redo it?"

## 8. Failure modes

- "What does a smart beginner get wrong at this step?"
- "What's the early tell that it's going sideways — before it's obviously broken?"
- "What's the expensive mistake here — the one that's hard to recover from?"

## Reframes when someone is stuck

- **Contrast:** "Tell me about a time this went badly. What was different?"
- **Teaching frame:** "Pretend you're training your replacement. What's the one thing you'd drill into them about this step?"
- **Extreme frame:** "What would a lazy/sloppy version look like, so we can name what *not* to do?"
- **Artifact frame:** "Do you have an example of the output from this step we can reverse-engineer?"
